**What you have:** A persona-led sports betting picks brand ("Hanoi" = the capper's alias, "Picks" = the product). A live Discord (free + VIP), 3 social accounts feeding it, and Whop queued for payments.

**Pricing target:** $30–50/mo VIP with a low-friction weekly trial. Kazu ($30/mo) is the floor; don't go GOAT-tier ($125) until there's a real face/following behind it.

**Your #1 gap right now:** No bios, no link-in-bio, no Discord invite on *any* social account. You're making 135 YouTube Shorts that funnel nowhere. Fixing the funnel is higher leverage than any logo.

## 1. Brand Foundation

### 1.1 The name
**Hanoi Picks.** "Hanoi" is the capper's nickname/alias (not a real name); "Picks" is the product. The name is genuinely an asset in this space — it's a *persona with a place-flavor*, distinctive against generic "[Word]Picks / [Word]Bets" competitors. It implies a character, not a faceless service. Lean into that.

The international/Vietnam flavor also hands you a ready-made color story (red + gold + star) that no competitor is using — turning the name into the visual identity instead of fighting it.

### 1.2 The persona — "Hanoi"
Treat Hanoi as a **character**, the way GOAT has Victor and PropFellas have their named cappers. The community trust research is blunt: a *named, consistent, face-or-mask-attached persona is already a differentiator* because anonymous rotating cappers read as scams.

Persona brief:
- **Archetype:** the calm, calculated sharp. Not a screaming hype-man, not a Lambo-flexer. Deliberate, data-first, a little mysterious.
- **One-liner:** "Calculated picks. Receipts on the table."
- **Voice:** confident, dry, numbers-forward. Talks in units and CLV, not "TRUST ME BRO."
- **Visual identity for the persona:** can be a real face, a stylized silhouette/mask, or an illustrated mascot (see logo options). A recurring character is white space — almost nobody in the category has one.

### 1.3 Positioning statement
> **Hanoi Picks is a transparent, process-first sports betting community for people tired of screenshot scammers — every pick posted before tip, every result tracked in public, run by a sharp who'd rather show the math than sell a dream.**

### 1.4 Brand pillars
1. **Transparency is the product.** Public verified record, losses included. This is the moat.
2. **Process over prophecy.** We teach the *why* — lines, units, CLV — so members keep the skill, not just the pick.
3. **Calculated, not hype.** No "locks," no rented-Lambo energy. Sharp, dry, premium.
4. **Community of serious bettors.** Not a pick-vending machine — a room of people who bet analytically.

### 1.5 Differentiation / category white space
From the competitor teardown, here's what *everyone* does — and where you break the pattern:

| Category default (everyone) | Hanoi Picks move |
|---|---|
| Near-black background, green/gold accent | Keep dark base, but **own deep red + gold** as the signature |
| Plain wordmark or single-letter logo | **Emblem/seal + a persona character** |
| Fire/money emojis doing the design work | A real graphic system (the pick-card already exists on IG) |
| Anonymous or rotating cappers | A named **character** ("Hanoi") with continuity |
| "70-80% win rate!!!" inflated claims | **Verified Pikkit record**, realistic numbers, units |
| Hype tone ("STOP LOSING START WINNING") | Calm, calculated, receipts-first |

### 1.6 Brand voice — do / don't
**Do say:** calculated, edge, value, units, CLV, +EV, process, transparent record, analysis, the card, tail, sweat.
**Never say (scam + legal flags):** lock, guaranteed, sure thing, insider, 100-unit play, GOAT, "get rich," "can't lose."

Internal Discord vernacular (degen / juice / tail / units) is fine and signals authenticity. Keep that slang **off** any cold-audience landing page or ad — there, the tone is clean and credible.

## 2. Visual Identity System

### 2.1 Logo direction
Drew likes **Kazu Picks'** logo and wants the same *feel* but differentiated. Kazu's mark is a **dark, minimal circular avatar with a stylized "K" lettermark / clean wordmark** — premium, tech-clean, no mascot. Good feel; the differentiation is owning a **color + an emblem/character** Kazu doesn't have.

**▶ RECOMMENDED — "The Star Seal" (emblem + wordmark)**
A minimal **circular coin/seal** on lacquer black, with a single **five-point gold star** as the centerpiece (the Hanoi/flag nod), and "HANOI PICKS" set in a bold condensed uppercase beneath. Reads like a premium seal/coin — keeps Kazu's dark-minimal premium feel, but the star + red ring make it instantly its own. Works as a circular profile pic on every platform.

**Alt A — Persona Mask/Mascot**
A stylized minimalist **character mark** for "Hanoi" — a confident, calm figure or a sharp masked silhouette. This is the biggest white-space grab (a recurring character nobody else has). Can be anime-adjacent if you want that energy. Higher build effort, highest brand stickiness.

**Alt B — Pure Wordmark**
"**HANOI**" large + "PICKS" small, condensed grotesque, gold-on-black, with a **single gold star dotting/replacing a letter** (e.g. the "i"). Cleanest, closest to category norm, fastest to ship. Good as a *secondary* horizontal logo even if you pick the Seal as primary.

> Production note: generative tools (Imagen/Flow) are great for *exploring* and for the mascot/persona art, but a final logo should be cleaned up / redrawn as a vector (text in AI-generated logos is usually garbled). Generate the mark, then trace it crisp.

### 2.2 Color palette
Own the red+gold lane. Hex values to lock in:

| Role | Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base background | Lacquer Black | `#0E0E10` | Discord-native dark, all backgrounds |
| **Primary brand** | **Hanoi Red** | `#C8102E` | The signature — accents, ring, headers, CTA |
| **Accent / premium** | **Saigon Gold** | `#E8B33A` | Star, VIP tier, premium highlights, dividers |
| Win / positive | Cash Green | `#2ECC71` | Results, "W", graded wins (functional only) |
| Loss / negative | Faded Red | `#7A2230` | Graded losses (muted, not alarming) |
| Body text | Rice Paper | `#F5F3EE` | Primary text on dark |
| Muted text | Ash | `#8A8A93` | Secondary text, timestamps |

Rule of thumb: **black + red + gold is the brand**; green/red only appear functionally on graded results so wins/losses read instantly.

### 2.3 Typography
- **Display / logo:** a bold **condensed grotesque** (e.g. Anton, Druk-style, Archivo Black, or Bebas Neue). All-caps, tight. This is the category's "Impact-adjacent" energy done with taste.
- **Headlines / embeds:** a clean geometric sans (Inter, Sora, or Satoshi) — premium and legible in Discord.
- **Body / pick cards:** Inter or system sans. Numbers must be tabular/monospaced where records and units are shown so columns align.
- Avoid: novelty fonts, scripts, anything that reads "casino."

### 2.4 Imagery style — the pick card
Your IG already has the strongest asset across all three accounts: **designed pick cards** (player photo + team/flag branding + "hanoi" name). Systematize it:
- Lacquer-black card, Hanoi-red accent bar, gold for the pick/odds line.
- Cutout player photo, league logo, the market + line + odds + units, confidence, and a small star-seal logo lockup in the corner.
- One consistent template per sport (swap accent imagery, keep layout). Consistency is what turns scattered posts into a recognizable brand.

### 2.5 Iconography & emotes
Build a small custom emote/icon set for Discord (replaces the generic fire/money emojis everyone leans on): gold-star ⭐ (brand), red "H" coin, a "W" coin (green) and "L" coin (muted) for results reactions, a "lock-in" stamp, a units chip. Custom emotes are cheap brand equity and make `results` channels look pro.

## Bettor Community & Trust Research

### Trust Signals to Build In
- **Third-party verified records are non-negotiable.** Screenshots mean nothing; only records linked to **Pikkit / BetStamp / Action Network** earn credibility. r/sportsbook daily threads cite Pikkit as the standard. Show a live Pikkit profile from day one, full ledger incl. losses.
- **Post picks before games start, with the exact line + book + timestamp.** Retroactive picks ("past-posting") are the fraudster signature.
- **Losing transparency is an asset.** Publishing a losing week with a writeup proves the operation is real; deleting/disappearing during losses gets called out instantly.
- **CLV (Closing Line Value)** is the sharpness indicator the community respects most — display it alongside W/L.
- **Report in units, not dollars.** "Up 34 units" reads credible; "$50,000 profit" reads as marketing.
- **Be transparent about how you make money** (memberships) — hiding the revenue model breeds suspicion.

### Red Flags to Avoid
- "100-unit locks" / guaranteed-win language (banned in r/sportsbook POTD threads).
- Screenshots as the only proof.
- Rented-Lambo / lifestyle flexing (explicitly mocked; "Degenerates United" won share by mocking it).
- Past-posting after line movement.
- DM-first recruiting (BBB 2025 "scamicapper" alert) — be inbound-only.
- Selling **both sides** of a pick to different groups.
- Disappearing/deleting during losing streaks.
- Anonymous/rotating capper identities (a named persona like "Hanoi" is already a differentiator).
- Inflated 70–80% win-rate claims (55–58% is already elite — realistic numbers convince more).

### What Paid Members Value
1. **Education on process**, not just picks ("the education changed how I see lines").
2. **CLV-positive alerts** — the +EV-literate cohort has the highest retention.
3. **Access to the capper** (AMA channels, direct engagement).
4. **Live betting alerts** (free tiers can't match the speed — strongest reason to pay).
5. **A community of serious/analytical bettors** (social retention is real).
6. **Bankroll management frameworks** (cited as tangible ROI).
7. **Multiple cappers with sport specialties** (anchor = Hanoi; verified guests for niche sports).

### Naming & Branding Conventions
- **Premium:** persona/place-led names (Hanoi Picks fits), understated ("Edges," "Sharp," "Value," "Network," "The [Noun]"), process-implying names.
